How It Works

THE SCIENCE OF
Evaporative COOLING

Cooling towers are specialized heat exchangers that bring water and air into direct or indirect contact to reduce water temperature through the natural process of evaporation.

01

HOT WATER DISTRIBUTION

Heated process water from industrial equipment, HVAC condensers, or power generation systems is pumped to the top of the cooling tower. Advanced nozzle or basin distribution systems spread the water uniformly across the fill media surface, maximizing the air-water contact area for efficient heat transfer.

02

AIR & WATER INTERACTION

As hot water cascades downward through specially designed PVC fill media, it creates a thin film with maximum surface exposure. Simultaneously, air is drawn through the tower — either mechanically by fans or naturally by convection. The counter-current or cross-current contact between air and water enables highly efficient thermal exchange.

03

EVAPORATIVE HEAT REJECTION

A small fraction of the circulating water (typically 1–2%) evaporates as it contacts the airstream. This evaporation absorbs latent heat from the remaining water, producing a significant cooling effect. The warm, moisture-laden air is then discharged from the tower through drift eliminators that capture entrained water droplets.

04

COOLED WATER RETURN

The cooled water collects in the tower basin and is pumped back to the process or HVAC equipment to absorb heat again, completing the closed-loop cycle. Make-up water is added to compensate for evaporation, drift, and blowdown losses, while water treatment systems maintain optimal water chemistry to prevent scale, corrosion, and biological growth.

Tower Types

COOLING TOWER
Configurations

We manufacture a complete range of cooling tower configurations to match every application's requirements for airflow, footprint, thermal performance, and maintenance accessibility.

CF

COUNTERFLOW TOWERS

In counterflow design, air travels vertically upward through the fill media, directly opposing the downward flow of water. This configuration delivers maximum thermal efficiency and the smallest footprint, as the coldest water contacts the driest incoming air at the base. Pressurized spray nozzles ensure uniform water distribution from above.

Highest Efficiency Compact Footprint No Sunlight on Fill
XF

CROSSFLOW TOWERS

Crossflow towers direct air horizontally across the vertically falling water stream. Hot water is distributed by gravity through basins at the top, eliminating the need for pressurized spray systems. This design offers excellent maintenance accessibility — all internal components are easily reachable — and lower pump head requirements, reducing energy costs.

Easy Maintenance Gravity Distribution Lower Pump Head
ID

INDUCED DRAFT TOWERS

Induced draft towers position the fan at the tower's discharge point, pulling air upward through the fill. This creates low entering and high exiting air velocities, significantly reducing the risk of recirculation — where warm exhaust air is drawn back into the tower. Available in both counterflow and crossflow configurations for maximum design flexibility.

Low Recirculation Energy Efficient Top-Mounted Fan
FD

FORCED DRAFT TOWERS

Forced draft systems use fans at the tower's air intake to push air through the structure. This design can handle high static pressure applications, making it suitable for indoor installations and ducted configurations where space is constrained. Centrifugal or axial fans are positioned at the base, forcing air upward through the fill media and water stream.

Indoor Suitable High Static Pressure Ductable
CL

CLOSED CIRCUIT TOWERS

Closed circuit (or fluid cooler) towers circulate the process fluid through a sealed coil inside the tower, preventing direct contact with the atmosphere. External spray water evaporates over the coil surface to provide cooling. This design protects sensitive fluids from contamination, eliminates water treatment complexity, and maintains fluid purity for critical process applications.

Fluid Protection No Contamination Sealed System
ND

NATURAL DRAFT TOWERS

Natural draft towers utilize the density difference between warm humid air inside the tower and cooler dry air outside to create a natural upward airflow — requiring no mechanical fans. The iconic hyperboloid shape maximizes structural efficiency and accelerates convective airflow. These towers are primarily used in large-scale power generation facilities where high cooling capacities are required.

No Fan Energy Very High Capacity Power Plants

FAQ

FREQUENTLY ASKED
Questions

What is a cooling tower and how does it work?+

A cooling tower is a heat rejection device that cools process water by evaporation. Hot water from industrial equipment or HVAC systems is pumped to the top of the tower and distributed over fill media. As ambient air passes through the tower, a small portion of the water evaporates, removing significant heat from the remaining water. The cooled water then returns to the process equipment to absorb heat again, completing a continuous cooling cycle.

What is the difference between counterflow and crossflow towers?+

In counterflow towers, air flows vertically upward through the fill media, directly opposing the downward flow of water. This maximizes thermal contact efficiency and allows for a more compact footprint. In crossflow towers, air flows horizontally across the falling water. Crossflow designs offer easier maintenance access and use gravity-fed water distribution, reducing pump energy requirements. The choice depends on your priorities: maximum efficiency (counterflow) or maximum maintainability (crossflow).

Why choose FRP over galvanized steel cooling towers?+

FRP (Fiberglass Reinforced Plastic) cooling towers offer several critical advantages over galvanized steel: complete corrosion immunity (including in coastal, chemical, and high-humidity environments), significantly lighter weight for easier installation, longer service life (50+ years vs. 15–20 for galvanized steel), lower lifecycle cost due to reduced maintenance, and no risk of white rust or red rust formation. FRP towers maintain structural integrity in environments that would rapidly degrade metal towers.

How do I determine the right cooling tower size?+

Cooling tower sizing depends on several factors: the heat load to be rejected (in BTU/hr or kW), required water flow rate (GPM or m³/h), desired cooled water temperature (cold water temperature), entering hot water temperature, and local wet bulb temperature conditions. Our engineering team will perform a detailed thermal analysis considering your specific site conditions, ambient climate data, and process requirements to specify the optimal tower capacity and configuration.

How do you prevent Legionella in cooling towers?+

Legionella prevention requires a multi-faceted approach: proper tower design with UV-blocking casing to prevent algae growth, steep basin slopes for positive drainage (eliminating stagnant water zones), high-efficiency drift eliminators to minimize aerosol discharge, and compatibility with chemical biocide or UV disinfection systems. Regular maintenance including periodic cleaning, water quality monitoring, and adherence to local health regulations is essential. Our towers are designed with Legionella prevention as a core engineering requirement.
